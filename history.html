Ancient & Medieval Music (Pre-1400s)

Music was primarily vocal, used in rituals and religious ceremonies.

Instruments like the lyre, flute, and drums were common.

Renaissance Music (1400s-1600s)

Polyphony became popular, with multiple independent melodies played together.

Composers like Palestrina and Monteverdi pioneered harmonic advancements.

Baroque Music (1600s-1750s)

Ornate melodies and complex compositions characterized this era.

Bach, Vivaldi, and Handel were major composers.

Classical Music (1750s-1820s)

Emphasis on clarity, balance, and structured forms like sonatas and symphonies.

Notable composers: Mozart, Beethoven, and Haydn.

Romantic Music (1820s-1900s)

Focused on emotional expression and dramatic themes.

Major composers: Chopin, Tchaikovsky, and Brahms.

20th Century & Modern Music

Experimentation with electronic sounds and diverse genres.

Jazz, rock, pop, and hip-hop emerged as dominant styles.
